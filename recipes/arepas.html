Arepas

Arepas are small grilled corn cakes and are native to Venezuela and Colombia

They can be eaten plain or stuffed with meats, cheese, beans and vegetables

  • 2 1/2 cups milk
  • 1 1/2 cups arepas flour
  • 1/2 cup grated Monterey Jack cheese
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  1. Bring milk to a simmer in a pot.
  2. Combine arepas flour, grated Monterey Jack cheese, kosher salt and black pepper in a large bowl
  3. Form dough into 12 balls about 2 inches in diameter.
  4. Heat oil in a nonstick skillet over medium heat.
